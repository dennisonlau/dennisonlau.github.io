In this project, I have designed a mixed signal system which can detect a change in capacitance from 1pF to 5pF in 0.18um CMOS technology. The image above shown that the system architecture comprises of two main blocks, the relaxtion oscillator which modulates the change in capacitance into frequency while the time-to-digital block converts the frequency of the wave into digital bits.

May 2020
All-Terrain Robotic Research Platform

This is my final year project at University of Southampton, under the supervision of Dr Klaus Peter Zauner. The aim of this project is to create a general off-road robotic platform based on Raspberry Pi 4 and Pixhawk 4 autopilot system that can be used for research purposes such as carrying payload/payloads. The final designed rover comes equiped with a Raspberry Pi 4 and a Pixhawk 4. The developed platform is capable of being controlled by an RC transmitter as well as by the on-board computer. It also offers multiple sensing capabilities such as individual motor current sensing, low battery voltage sensing, and temperature sensing. One key aspect that makes this platform different from other commercially available robotic platform is the design of the payload interface which makes it easy for the user to integrate sensors or payloads into the platform.

This project involved the design and implementation of the entire hardware and software system architecture. The hardware itself is divided into two level, low-level hardware(pyboard) in which the control of the motor drivers and onboard sensors interfacing is done and high-level hardware (Raspberry Pi 4) in which the user can program. ROS Melodic is implemented on the Raspberry Pi 4 to faciliate the communication and data transfer between the Pyboard and the Pixhawk 4.
